# Stage 2 — Research, Trends & Strategy Reference

## Purpose

Stage 2 is the skill's intellectual engine. A proposal that does not rest on cultural observation, precedent, and synthesis is decoration. The client does not need more decoration; they can get that anywhere. They need to know that Bold sees the moment clearly and has chosen this direction *because* of what is happening in the world.

Stage 2 produces three artifacts:
- `trends.md`, what is moving in culture right now that this event should ride.
- `case-studies.md`, specific events/activations that solved adjacent challenges well.
- `inspiration.md`, a creative mood board in prose: references, motifs, tensions.

All three are short, sharp, and sourced. Padding is disqualifying.

---

## Timing

Give this stage real research time. Budget ~15 to 30 `web_search` + `web_fetch` calls. Complex or high-stakes briefs justify more. Save good sources; they will be cited in the proposal PDF.

---

## Trends, the methodology

### What counts as a trend

A trend for Bold's purposes is a pattern that is (a) visible across at least 3 independent sources, (b) active in the past 24 months, (c) relevant to either the audience, the format, or the message of this specific event. A one-off viral moment is not a trend; a fashion magazine's opinion is not a trend unless other evidence confirms it.

### What does NOT count

- "AI is hot right now", too broad to be useful.
- "Sustainability matters", a stance, not a trend.
- A single viral TikTok, noise.
- Anything from a content-marketing blog selling a service, biased source.

### Where to look

**Global event / experience trends:**
- Event Marketer, Bizzabo, Cvent, Eventex blogs and award lists
- Campaign, It's Nice That, Creative Review
- Dezeen, Frame, Archdaily (for spatial / scenographic trends)
- TrendHunter, WGSN (if accessible), Business of Fashion (for luxury-event trends)
- Cannes Lions, Clio, D&AD, One Show winners (past 24 months)
- SXSW, Cannes, Davos recap coverage

**Israeli event / culture trends:**
- Globes, Calcalist, TheMarker event/lifestyle sections
- Xnet lifestyle, Haaretz Gallery
- Israeli design festival coverage (Jerusalem Design Week, Houses from Within)
- Instagram accounts of leading Israeli event producers and venues

**Adjacent signals (worth searching):**
- Luxury hospitality trends (hotel openings, bar programs)
- Fashion week stagings
- Museum exhibition design
- Nightlife (what clubs/parties look like in Berlin, London, Tel Aviv)

### Output format for trends.md

```markdown
# טרנדים רלוונטיים, [שם אירוע]

## מבוא (2-3 שורות)
למה דווקא הטרנדים האלה רלוונטיים לאתגר שזיהינו בשלב 1.

## טרנד 1: [שם קצר, 2-4 מילים]
**מה זה:** 1-2 משפטים.
**ראיות:** [3 מקורות עם קישורים].
**למה לנו:** איך זה עונה למתח שזוהה ב-challenges.md.
**איך להביא את זה לאירוע:** 1-2 משפטים קונקרטיים.

## טרנד 2: ...
## טרנד 3: ...
(3 עד 5 טרנדים סך הכל)

## אנטי-טרנד: מה להימנע
דפוס אחד או שניים שלמרות הפופולריות שלהם לא מתאימים לאירוע הזה, ולמה.
```

### Quality bar

Each trend must have:
- At least 3 independent sources (not 3 reposts of the same original).
- A direct line back to a challenge from stage 1. If you cannot write "this trend answers challenge X", drop it.
- A concrete "how to express it" sentence. If the trend cannot be expressed at an event, it does not belong here.

## Case studies, the methodology

### What you are looking for

Specific named events (not genres) that solved an adjacent challenge in a memorable way. Adjacency beats identity: the best case study for a product launch might be a museum opening, not another product launch.

### Sources

- **Bold's own archive**: if the user grants Drive access, pull from it first. Internal precedent is strongest.
- **Award show winners**: Event Marketer's Ex Awards, Cannes Lions Experience category, Eventex Awards, Clio Entertainment, SEGD.
- **Agency portfolios**: Jack Morton, George P. Johnson, Momentum, MKTG, Creative Technology, Stark, Brink (Israel), Arnon Zadok (Israel), Bold's known competitors.
- **Coverage**: recap articles on specific events, behind-the-scenes photo essays.
- **Social**: event producers' Instagram case reels; look for events with published budgets or press mentions that indicate scale.

### Output format for case-studies.md

```markdown
# Case Studies רלוונטיים, [שם אירוע]

## Case 1: [שם האירוע / שם הלקוח / שנה]
- **מה נעשה:** 2-3 משפטים.
- **המפיקים / המעצבים:** (אם ידוע)
- **התוצאה:** מספר, ציטוט, או תוצאה מתועדת.
- **מקור:** [קישור / קישורים]
- **מה נשאל מכאן:** רעיון קונקרטי אחד.
- **מה לא נשאל:** רעיון קונקרטי שנדחה ולמה.

## Case 2: ...
## Case 3: ...
(3 עד 5 מקרים)

## Case פנימי (אם רלוונטי): [אירוע קודם של Bold]
ציטוט מהצעה ישנה מ-Drive של Bold, עם הפניה לקובץ המקור.
```

### Quality bar

- Each case study is a real, named event. "Some tech conference in Austin" fails; "SXSW 2024 Netflix Stranger Things activation" passes.
- Each case study has a verifiable source.
- At least one of the 3+ case studies must be Israeli if the event is Israeli. Local precedent carries disproportionate weight with Israeli clients.

## Inspiration, the methodology

This is the softer sibling of trends and case studies. It captures visual, sensory, and conceptual references that will seed stage 3 (Brand Heart). It is deliberately more associative, more poetic. But it is still sourced.

### What belongs here

- Artists, designers, photographers, filmmakers whose work captures the event's feeling.
- Specific artworks, films, books, albums, or scenes.
- Material references (a specific marble, a specific light quality, a specific fabric).
- Historical periods or movements (Bauhaus austerity / Memphis playfulness / Japanese wabi-sabi / Mediterranean modernism).
- Non-event references that carry the right energy: a particular restaurant's plating, a hotel lobby, a film's opening title sequence.

### Output format for inspiration.md

```markdown
# מצפן השראה, [שם אירוע]

## הרגשה ברגע אחד (one-sentence vibe)
משפט אחד שתופס את התחושה.

## מקור חזותי 1: [שם]
- **מה זה:** [ציטוט פריים, סצינה, עבודה, חלל]
- **למה זה מתאים:** 1-2 משפטים
- **מה לקחת מזה:** אלמנט קונקרטי לאירוע (צבע, חומר, הרכב, תנועה)
- **לינק / קרדיט:** [מקור]

(5 עד 10 מקורות, חזותיים, קולנועיים, מוזיקליים, ארכיטקטוניים, קולינריים)

## מתחים לשמור עליהם (tensions to preserve)
- מתח 1: [למשל "אינטימי אבל גרנדיוזי", "בלתי-טקסי אבל מדויק"]
- מתח 2: ...
שני עד שלושה מתחים. מתחים הם מה שנותן לאירוע חיים. ללא מתח, העיצוב הופך שטוח.

## Mood words (10-15)
מילים בודדות שתופסות את הפלטה הרגשית. מיועדות להיכנס בהמשך לפרומפטים של nano-banana.
```

### Quality bar

- Each reference must be nameable. "Some Scandinavian interior" fails; "Vincent Van Duysen's Graanmarkt 13" passes.
- At least 7 of 10 references must not be from the event-production world. The risk in stage 2 is inbreeding: if all your inspiration is other events, the output will look like other events. Import from fashion, film, architecture, cuisine.
- Mood words must be in Hebrew if the event is Hebrew-speaking.

---

## Synthesis check before stage 3

Before closing stage 2, answer in one paragraph for yourself (keep in working notes, not in a delivered artifact):

> *"If this event were a sentence, what would it be?"*

Write the sentence. If you can't, stage 2 is not done. Stage 3 depends on having this thread.
