## 4. Voice

Bold's *corporate* voice (distinct from any given event's tone). Typically:
- Confident, not boastful
- Specific, not generic
- Considered, not hurried
- Hebrew-first in Israel, English for international clients

"We" is used sparingly. Prefer active sentences. Avoid "solutions", "partner", "journey", "passionate".

---

## 5. Photography & imagery treatment

- Warm documentary style for event photos
- High contrast, controlled shadows
- People visible but rarely the main subject, the *space* is the subject
- Never stock. Only commissioned or event-derived imagery.

## 6. Application to proposal PDFs

- Cover logo: bottom-right corner, 20mm wide, at 80% opacity if over a busy visual

- Section dividers: thin horizontal line in accent color
- Quote/emphasis blocks: a 4mm-wide vertical line in accent color at the right edge of the block (RTL)

## How event-specific brand hearts relate to this

For each proposal, a fresh brand heart is created in `03-brand-heart/brand-system.md`. That system is the *event's* identity, built on top of Bold's corporate identity. The event palette may be entirely different from Bold's palette. But Bold's logo, voice, and typography choices for Bold-authored sections (the "About Bold" page, the footer, the contact block) always come from this file.

Translation rule: if the client asks "how does this proposal look like Bold?", the answer is in the *quality and restraint*, not in color matching. Every event has its own palette. Only the logo and the voice are universal.
